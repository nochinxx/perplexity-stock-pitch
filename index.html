Risk Assessment

The Honest Case Against

1

Serial Guidance Cuts Destroy Credibility

Three consecutive guidance cuts: FY2024 $37.20 → FY2025 ~$33 → FY2026 guide $30 → current $25.50.

Rebuttal: FY2026 floor of $25.50 explicitly excludes $3.75/share of non-recurring favorable items from FY2025 — the YoY decline is overstated. Management reaffirmed guidance March 10, including sanctions impact.
2

OCF Collapsed 47% in 2 Years

$8.1B (FY2023) → $5.8B (FY2024) → $4.3B (FY2025). Cash generation is deteriorating.

Rebuttal: FY2025 OCF was depressed by timing of Medicaid payments received in early January 2026. Management guided OCF floor of $5.5B for FY2026, implying 28% recovery. FY2023 was the peak before Medicaid utilization surge.
3

CMS Sanctions — Unquantifiable Exposure

Potential multi-billion clawback if risk-adjustment data was systematically inflated for 7 years (since Nov 2018).

Rebuttal: Issue is specifically about submitting corrections via USB drives instead of electronic systems — a procedural dispute, not fraud. CMS extended the deadline to May 30 and exempted 11 of 45 contracts. Management frames it as a policy dispute about how retroactive corrections should be treated.
4

Medicaid -1.75% Margin May Not Be Trough

"Trough year" has been wrong for 2 consecutive years. Why should we believe it now?

Rebuttal: Medicaid redetermination — the structural driver of adverse selection — is now largely complete. Rate actualization lags 12–18 months. 2026 rates reflect more current utilization experience. Management targets at least 12% EPS growth in 2027 off the 2026 base.
5

Health Benefits OI Down 39% in 2 Years

$6.9B (FY2023) → $4.2B (FY2025). Core insurance profitability is eroding.

Rebuttal: Carelon OI grew from $2.9B to $3.4B over the same period (+17%). Total company OI decline is less severe than Health Benefits alone, and the growth engine is shifting toward Carelon's higher-margin services.
